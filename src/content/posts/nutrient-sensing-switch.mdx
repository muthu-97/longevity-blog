<p class="lede">Two members, same age, same gym. One is lean and energetic, the other carries a soft middle and crashes by evening, despite eating "the same". The difference often comes down to one tiny switch every cell is flipping all day long.</p>

Every cell constantly asks: **is there food around?** The answer flips it between two modes:

- **Grow & store**, switched on by eating, especially lots of protein and sugar. The body builds, stores, gets bigger.
- **Repair & clean**, switched on by fasting, training, and being a little hungry. The body fixes damage and takes out the trash.

You need both. Growth builds muscle; repair keeps you young. The problem is **modern life jams the switch on "grow"**, eating from morning to midnight, barely moving, so the repair mode almost never runs.

Stuck on "grow" for years, the body stops listening properly, the start of insulin resistance, belly fat, type 2 diabetes, and faster aging. But the fix isn't to live in permanent repair either (that's its own problem: lost muscle, weak bones, crashed hormones). Risk sits at **both** ends. The whole game is cycling.

## The proper names

<dl class="terms">
  <dt>Nutrient sensing</dt>
  <dd>The cell's system for detecting food and energy, and switching between growth and repair accordingly. <span class="more"><a href="/glossary/#nutrient-sensing">definition →</a></span></dd>
  <dt>mTOR</dt>
  <dd>The master "grow" switch, turned up by protein and feeding. Great for building muscle, harmful when always on. <span class="more"><a href="/glossary/#mtor">definition →</a></span></dd>
  <dt>AMPK</dt>
  <dd>The "repair / energy-low" switch, turned up by exercise and fasting. The counterweight to mTOR. <span class="more"><a href="/glossary/#ampk">definition →</a></span></dd>
  <dt>Insulin resistance</dt>
  <dd>When cells stop responding to insulin's "store this" signal, the body shouts louder (high insulin), the start of metabolic disease. <span class="more"><a href="/glossary/#insulin-resistance">definition →</a></span></dd>
  <dt>HOMA-IR</dt>
  <dd>A simple calculation from fasting glucose and insulin that estimates insulin resistance. <span class="more"><a href="/glossary/#homa-ir">definition →</a></span></dd>
</dl>
